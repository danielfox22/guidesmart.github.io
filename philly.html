hi, welcome to Philadelphia

Philadelphia, Pennsylvania’s largest city, is notable for its rich history, on display at the Liberty Bell, Independence Hall, and other American Revolutionary sites.

The Liberty bell

The Liberty Bell now residing in a huge glass gazebo, this 2,080-pound piece of history was once mounted in the belfry of Independence Hall. Historians believe the first crack developed in the early 1840s. Metal workers were tasked with repairing the bell in anticipation of George Washington's birthday in 1846. The repair was unsuccessful and the bell ceased to chime ever again.

Independence hall

It was here in Independence Hall that the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution were debated, drafted and signed by our forefathers. You can also see surviving copies of the declaration, the Constitution and the Articles of Confederation, all displayed in the West Wing's Great Essentials Exhibit.

Philadelphia Museum of Art

Whether you're actually interested in the exhibits or just looking to re-enact that timeless scene from "Rocky," the Philadelphia Museum of Art is a must-see. Once inside you can dive right into the museum's impressive of works by Matisse, Van Gogh, Picasso, Cézanne and many others.

EASTERN STATE PENITENTIARY

The castle-like Eastern State Penitentiary is one of Philadelphia's most important historic sites. Having opened in 1829, this prison sought to reform prisoners using isolation and reflection rather than capital punishment and physical abuse. The Eastern State Penitentiary led the way to reform in the judicial system. Today, you are welcome to tour the facility; the highlight for many is Al Capone's cell.

TIMELINE of Philly

Philadelphia is the largest city in the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania in the United States. It is the sixth-most-populous city in the United States and the most populous city in the state of Pennsylvania, with a 2020 population of 1,603,797.

1600s-Early 1700s 
The Founding of Philly

William Penn was granted a charter by King Charles of England in 1681. Despite the Royal Charter, Penn bought the land from the local Lenape to be on good terms with the Native Americans and ensure peace for his colony. In 1701 the city of Philadelphia was officialy incorporated and became an official town.

1770-1790
The American Revolution

Philadelphia was a hotbed for the American Revolution. In 1774 The Continental Congress met briefly in Carpenter's Hall. The second Continental Congress (1775-1781) met, and this was when the Declaration of Independence was written, saying that the United Colonies wanted to be seperate from British rule.   

1900a
Modern Philly forming 

The 1900s in Philadelphia experienced massive growth with the population reaching over 2 million people by the 1950s. Many of the modern day buildings that you see in Philadelphia today can be traced back to the 1900s.

Current Day
Philly Today

The Modern Philadelphia is one that is of rich tradition and culture. This city has produced well known people from founding fathers to musicians. Every turn you take here, you are looking back into Americas past.
