hi, welcome to orlando

Orlando, a city in central Florida, is home to more than a dozen theme parks. Chief among its claims to fame is Walt Disney World, comprised of parks like the Magic Kingdom and Epcot, as well as water parks. Another major destination, Universal Orlando, offers Universal Studios and Islands of Adventure, with the Wizarding World of Harry Potter straddling both‚Äù

Walt disney world

Southwest of Downtown Orlando, Walt Disney World occupies 25,000 acres of which half has been used.  There are 4 theme parks, 2 water parks, 34 hotels, and an outdoor shopping center. Each park offers a unique experience, from Star Wars to princesses, there is something for everyone at Walt Disney World.  

universal studios

Founded in 1990 as a direct competitor to Walt Disney World, this entertainment resort complex occupies 551 acres of land. Universal allows visitors to experience some of the largest film franchises in person, such as Harry Potter, Transformers, Jurrasic Park, and The Fast and The Furious. There are 2 parks, 8 hotels, and a shopping district.

Lake eola

Located in the heart of Downtown Orlando, with a sidewalk that circles the lake 0.9 miles in length, making it easy for visitors to keep track of their walking or running distances. Visitors can rent paddle boats, view the animals at the park, or even catch a concert.

leu gardens

The gardens contain nearly 50 acres of landscaped grounds and lakes, with meandering trails shaded by 200-year-old oaks and forests of camellias. You can take a lovely trail walk, enjoy a nice picnic, or even tour the house on property.

TIMELINE of Orlando

Orlando is one of the most-visited cities in the world primarily due to tourism, major events, and convention traffic; in 2018, the city drew more than 75 million visitors.

1800s
The Founding and Incorporation of Orlando 

In 1845 Mosquito County becomes modern day Orange County. In 1857 the town of Jernigan renames to Orlando. In 1875 Orlando becomes an incorporated town with 85 residents, with the citrus industry booming.

1960s
Orlando expansion

In 1965 Walt Disney unveils his plans for a theme park in Central Florida. The first attractions included the Haunted Mansion, the Mad Tea Party and Mr. Toad's Wild Ride. Three years later, in 1968 Florida Technical University (now known as UCF) opens its doors to almost 2,000 students.

1980s-1990s
Orlando Population Boom

The Orlando area reaches 164,000 by 1990. Many business and organizations are founded, suchas Orange County Convention Center, Amway Arena, Orlando Philharmonic Orchestra, etc. This is where you start to see the current day Orlando area form.   

Current Day
Modern Day Orlando

The city of Orlando population reaches over 270,000 people. The theme parks start expanding further, with multiple different attractions and areas opening. Orlando City Soccer forms, and competes in USL and MLS. With all the expansion in Orlando, there is so much to do, you cannot fit it all in one trip.
