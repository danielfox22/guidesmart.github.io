hi, welcome to Luxor

One of the oldest inhabited cities in the world, Luxor is a hugely popular visitor site, known as the “world’s greatest open-air museum”.

Karnak Temple

The Karnak temple complex at Luxor developed over more than 1,000 years, principally between the Twelfth and Twentieth Dynasties.

Banana Island

Located Along The Nile River Valley, Banana Island (Gezira El-Mozh) Attracts Visitors With Its Small Banana Plantation, Where You Can Learn About How They Are Being Kept And The Important Care Given To Their Growth.

Luxor Bridge To Kings Island

As an alternative to the Corniche (or for a longer run), a decent option is to run to Jazirat Al Awamiyyah (Kings Island), an island on the Nile just south of Luxor.

Museum luxor

A museum featuring items found beneath the Luxor Temple, and from the tomb of King Tut. Also on exhibit is a re-assembled wall from the Temple of Aten.

TIMELINE of Luxor

Luxor was the ancient city of Thebes, the great capital of Upper Egypt during the New Kingdom, and the glorious city of Amun, later to become the god Amun-Ra.

3200 BC
Thebes was inhabited around this time.

At this time it was still a small trading post, while Memphis served as the royal residence of the Old Kingdom pharaohs. Although no buildings survive in Thebes older than portions of the Karnak temple complex that may date from the Middle Kingdom.

1500 BC
IThebes is the Capital

Ahmose I founded a new age for a unified Egypt with Thebes as its capital. The city remained as capital during most of the 18th Dynasty (New Kingdom).With Egypt stabilized again, religion and religious centers flourished and none more so than Thebes.

November 1922
